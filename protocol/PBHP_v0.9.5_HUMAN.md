Pause Before Harm Protocol (PBHP) v0.9.5 — HUMAN

**This document supersedes all previous versions of PBHP-HUMAN. Running an outdated version of PBHP is not PBHP compliance. If you are unsure which version you are running, check github.com/PauseBeforeHarmProtocol/pbhp for the current release.**

Practical checklist for humans under uncertainty, power imbalance, or time pressure

Purpose & Scope
PBHP-HUMAN is a harm-reduction decision checklist. It is designed to interrupt confident, rushed, or incentive-distorted decisions from causing avoidable harm. PBHP does not decide what is morally right. PBHP determines whether it is safe to proceed, under what constraints, and with what accountability.

Human Sovereignty
This protocol does not override human agency. It introduces friction, not authority. Responsibility for action remains with the human at all times.

Harm Threshold (Activation Line in the Sand)
"Harm," in PBHP, refers to actions or guidance with a credible risk of irreversible physical injury, death, or permanent loss of autonomy. Emotional discomfort, ideological disagreement, or symbolic offense alone do not constitute harm under this threshold. Other harms (economic, reputational, psychological) can still be mapped, but PBHP reserves its strongest friction for irreversible or autonomy-destroying outcomes.

Note (v0.9.5): Job loss, coercive debt, doxxing, medical denial, and similar harms count as autonomy-destroying cascades when they trap exit under power imbalance. A single instance may be moderate; but cascades that erase agency are ORANGE minimum.

**Interpersonal & Content Moderation Note (v0.9.6):** In moderation contexts, sustained harassment, abuse, or intimidation patterns that would cause a reasonable person to withdraw from public space meet the harm threshold even without a single catastrophic event.

Target runtime & output
Typical runtime: 2–5 minutes (use PBHP-MIN for ≤30 seconds). Output labels: Proceed / Constrain / Modify / Stop.

When to Run PBHP-HUMAN (Quick Triggers)
- Health, safety, legal status, or rights
- Major money, job, or reputation impact
- Naming real people or vulnerable groups
- Irreversible, automated, or mass-distributed action
- You feel rushed, angry, euphoric, or "certain"
- You think: "This probably doesn't need a pause" (that thought is itself a trigger)

PBHP-HUMAN Checklist

Step 0 — Pause & Posture
Urgency (0–10): ____    Emotion present: anger / fear / excitement / certainty / none
Say (internally): "I may be wrong. I will not trade speed for harm."
Mode (pick one): EXPLORE (multiple plausible frames) / COMPRESS (one clean summary) / BOTH (explore then compress with disclosure)
Posture (priority order): Truth first. Protect the lesser. Reduce harm. Pause. Stay grounded in reality.
**"Truth first" means "ground your analysis in reality before acting," not "truth overrides other commitments." All commitments operate simultaneously. The posture order is a sequence of engagement, not a hierarchy of override.**

Crisis shortcut (v0.9.5): If this is a genuine crisis (lives at stake, delay = harm), prioritize: (1) Protect the most vulnerable, (2) Use the best info you have, (3) Preserve options, (4) Write it down after.
You MUST come back and run the full checklist once the crisis is over. "Crisis" does not mean "stressful" — it means people get hurt if you wait.
**Crisis Timeout (72 hours):** Crisis priority expires automatically after 72 hours. After 72 hours, full protocol reassessment is mandatory regardless of whether the crisis feels ongoing. Failure to reassess is drift.

Door Quality (v0.9.5): D1 = preview/confirm (for YELLOW). D2 = consent+rollback (for ORANGE). D3 = restructure choice (for severe asymmetry).

Step 3 — Agency Check (No-Choice Detector)
- ☐ Constraints acknowledged
- ☐ "No choice / we have to" claim questioned
- ☐ At least one alternative exists (delay, refuse, narrow scope, seek review, seek consent)
- ☐ Power-Inversion Test (v0.9.5): "If I had zero power and was on the receiving end of this decision, would I think it was fair?" If no → find a stronger safeguard or reconsider.
If you cannot name an alternative, default to pause or refusal. Inevitability is a warning sign, not a justification.

Step 5A — Dignity Check (v0.9.5)
For each person who could be harmed, ask:
- Do they have a real choice? (yes / no)
- Are they getting a fair deal? (yes / no)
- Is this the least intrusive way to do it? (yes / no)
- Can it be undone if it goes wrong? (yes / no)
- Could you explain this to them in plain language? (yes / no)
If 3+ answers are "no" for anyone → STOP and redesign the action before proceeding.

Step 6 — Risk Classification & Deterministic Gate→Action Mapping (v0.9.5)
- GREEN: low stakes, reversible → ACTION: PROCEED normally
- YELLOW: moderate risk → ACTION: PROCEED WITH MITIGATIONS (list warnings/limits)
- ORANGE: severe or power-asymmetric risk → ACTION: CONSTRAIN (safeguards + ≥D2 Door + alternatives)
- RED: severe + irreversible → ACTION: REFUSE/DELAY (require extraordinary justification; document why alternatives fail)
- BLACK: catastrophic/system-level → ACTION: REFUSE ABSOLUTELY (only critique/prevention allowed)
Power rule: if Power asymmetry = YES and Irreversible = YES → minimum ORANGE.
Escalation: if Power asymmetry = YES and Irreversible = YES and (Impact = severe or catastrophic) → minimum RED.
**Dual-Assessor Requirement (v0.9.6):** For decisions affecting >100 people or involving irreversible outcomes, get a second independent assessor. If disagreement is 2+ levels, escalate to the higher gate and document the disagreement.
**GREEN Logging Floor (v0.9.6):** For any decision involving power asymmetry or affecting >10 people, YELLOW is the minimum logging threshold regardless of gate. This prevents evidence-free decisions.
Accumulation Gate (v0.9.5): if individually GREEN steps chain into irreversible/power-asymmetric harm → escalate to ORANGE minimum.
Chosen class (circle one): GREEN / YELLOW / ORANGE / RED / BLACK

Multimodal Check (v0.9.5, updated v0.9.6)
If you're reading someone's tone, body language, or facial expressions: Did they confirm your read? If not, go by what they actually said, not what you think they meant.
**Non-inference default (v0.9.6):** Don't assign emotion or intent from non-verbal signals unless the subject confirmed it. Profiling from multimodal signals = ORANGE minimum.

Data Freshness Check (v0.9.5, updated v0.9.6)
Is any of your information old enough that it might have changed? If the answer matters for your decision, verify before acting. If you can't verify, say so and be more cautious.
**Temporal tagging (v0.9.6):** Tag information with freshness: "Accurate as of [date]" or "Last verified [date]; may have changed."

Drift Alarms (If any appear, rerun PBHP and round risk up)
- "It's temporary"
- "We have to" / "No choice"
- "It's just policy / just advice"
- "It's legal, so it's fine"
- "For the greater good"
- "We can fix it later"
- "Everyone does it"
- "It's targeted / only affects bad people"
- "It's obvious"
- "I'm just interpreting"
- "No need to verify"
Forced-motion red flags: secrecy demands, "do it now," punishing questions, bypassing review, or rewarding speed over verification.

False Positive Release Valve (Pause Justification Review)
Any pause may be challenged with: "Was this pause justified?" PBHP must answer: (1) what triggered the pause (specific gate/alarm), (2) what irreversible/autonomy risk was identified, (3) what safeguard/Door would allow safe continuation, and (4) what evidence/safeguard would have prevented the pause.

**Rate-Limiting (v0.9.6):** If the same actor challenges more than 3 pauses in a review period and all were rejected, the pattern of challenges itself is a drift alarm. Log the pattern and escalate. Systematically challenged pauses that are later validated = functioning safety system, not overreaction.

**Calibration Reminder System (v0.9.6):** After 30 days from first use, this checklist reminds you that monthly calibration is due. The reminder repeats daily until you perform and log calibration.

**Outcome-Based Drift Detection (v0.9.6):** Phrase-based alarms can be circumvented. If your decisions consistently produce gates lower than an independent reviewer would assign, that pattern (regardless of language) is drift. Review trigger: >80% GREEN across 20+ decisions involving power asymmetry.
